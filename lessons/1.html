Chapter 1

 

present tense verbs: -නවා -navaa

three preceding vowels: -anavaa / -inavaa / -enavaa

exception: bonavaa, dannavaa

used for both singular and plural.

usage:

ex) මං යනවා (I come)

1) I am going 2) I go habitually (on Tuesdays) 3) I will go soon.

word order: variable

තාත්තා පුටුව හදනවා. /taatta puṭuva hadanavaa./ Father is mending the chair

අම්මා දැන් තේ බොනවා. /ammaa dæn tee bonavaa./ Mother is drinking tea now.

no equivalent for English 'you'

between friends and intimates: ඔයා oyaa (that person)

addressing a superior or stranger: 3rd person

තාත්තාට ඕනැද? /taattaaṭa oonæda?/ Does father want it? (Do you want it, father?)

ගුනපාල මහත්තයා එහම හිතනවාද? /gunapaala mahattayaa ehema hitanavaada?/ Does Mr. Gunapaala think so? (Do you think so, Mr. Gunapaala?)

for plural: ඔය ගොල්ල oya golla (those people)

මහත්තයා used as 'you' (for males) in all formal cases: මහත්තයා හොඳට නින්ද ගියාද ඊයෙ? /mahattayaa hon̆daṭa ninda giyaada iiye?/ Did Mr. (you) sleep well last night?

හදනවා for try: තේ හදනවා. /tee hadanavaa./ make -> Pour out / try tea.

තේ වක්කරනවා. /tee vakkaranavaa./ pour -> Pour / make tea.

බලනවා for try: තේ බලනවා. /tee balanavaa./ look / examine / look after -> Try tea.

මිනිහා පුටුව හදනවා.
